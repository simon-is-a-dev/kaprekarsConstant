Kaprekar's Constant

D. R. Kaprekar
1905 – 1986  ·  Devlali, India

A schoolteacher who spent his evenings chasing numerical curiosities purely for the joy of it — no degree, no funding, no institutional backing. His peers dismissed him. He published in obscure journals anyway, describing self-descriptive numbers, Harshad numbers, and the constant that bears his name. Martin Gardner eventually wrote about him in Scientific American, and the world caught up. He proved that mathematics is, at its heart, a form of play.
